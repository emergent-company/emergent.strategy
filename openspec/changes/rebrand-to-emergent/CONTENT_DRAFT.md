## FAQ Section

### Question 1: What is Emergent?

**Answer**: Emergent is a living knowledge system that automatically organizes your organization's information. Instead of manually managing documents and folders, you simply add content and Emergent understands it, connects it to related concepts, and makes it instantly searchable. It's like having a brilliant research assistant who reads everything and knows exactly what you need.

### Question 2: How does it stay up-to-date automatically?

**Answer**: Every time you add new content, Emergent processes it in real-time—understanding the concepts, finding connections to existing knowledge, and updating your entire knowledge graph. There's no rebuild step, no manual indexing. Your knowledge base evolves as fast as your domain does.

### Question 3: Do I need to organize or tag content manually?

**Answer**: No. Emergent automatically understands the content and context of your documents. Relationships form naturally based on semantic meaning, not manual categorization. You focus on adding knowledge; Emergent handles the organization.

### Question 4: Can my whole team use it?

**Answer**: Yes. Emergent supports multiple organizations and projects, so you can organize knowledge by team, department, or client. Everyone works from the same living knowledge base with appropriate access controls. No more knowledge silos.

### Question 5: What types of content can I add?

**Answer**: Documents (PDF, Word, PowerPoint, Excel), web pages (via URL), Markdown files, plain text, and HTML. Emergent extracts the content, understands it, and makes it part of your searchable knowledge base.

### Question 6: How do I find information?

**Answer**: You can ask questions through the chat interface, but Emergent also works proactively—surfacing relevant insights, connections, and context as you work. It's not just reactive search; it's an intelligent system that knows what matters and brings it to your attention automatically.
